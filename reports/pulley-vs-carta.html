Analysis
Key Takeaways

1. Carta is rebuilding, not expanding. They peaked at 427 roles in Q3 2021 and cratered to 32 after four layoff rounds. At 21% of peak, this is a measured recovery. 6 turnaround signals and 5 CRO-reporting roles confirm sales leadership restructuring. 63 of those 88 roles were batch-posted Feb 1, likely a fiscal year plan or ATS refresh.

2. Carta's People team was neglected for two years. Zero People roles open from Q3 2022 through Q1 2024, during the worst of the layoffs. Now they're hiring 8 People roles (their largest cohort ever), all senior. Pulley's People function was built from scratch since March 2024 and has zero open roles today.

3. Remote-first is a talent moat. Carta requires relocation to SF, NYC, London, or SLC for every role. Pulley is 100% remote with engineering comp within 7%. Only 58% of Carta employees would recommend working there (vs 62% at Pulley), and fewer than half have a positive business outlook.

4. Carta's employer brand is a liability. Glassdoor 3.6 (down from mid-4s), CEO approval 55%, Fortune expose on toxic culture, data misuse scandal, gender discrimination complaints, equity worth 53% less than peak. Every senior candidate Googles this. Pulley's stability, full salary disclosure, and smaller scale are advantages.

5. Pulley's weakness: comp perception. Carta scores 4.1 on Glassdoor Comp & Benefits vs Pulley's 3.8. In the four head-to-head roles (Go backend, React frontend, channel sales, product marketing), Carta's comp ceiling is higher. Pulley wins on remote flexibility, first-hire ownership, and stability. Know which candidates value which.

6. Carta's People comp sets the benchmark. Director of HR at $225K–$300K (SF-only). Sr. HRBPs at $139K–$164K. Sr. TA Partners at $106K–$165K. These are the numbers Pulley will be compared against. Monitor these bands as you plan 2026 comp reviews.
